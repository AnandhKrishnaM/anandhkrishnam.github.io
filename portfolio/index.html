The Singularity: About Me

Research Thesis & Interests

I am a 5th-year BS-MS student in Physics at IISER Pune. My Master's thesis investigates the Bondi-Metzner-Sachs (BMS) group of asymptotic symmetries and their direct physical consequence: the Gravitational Memory Effect. This work involves:

  • Understanding the conformal structure of the spacetime, especially the asymptotically flat spacetime and its implications (The Carollian case).
  • Exploring the relation between soft gravitons, asymptotic symmetries, and IR physics. (Briefly acquaint myself with research avenues in conjuction with high energy methods in GR applications understanding the nature of gravity)
  • Utilizing the powerful xAct computer algebra framework for tensor calculus in curved spacetime. Developed a framework capable of doing computations in higher dimensional spacetimes with certain symmetries

My broader research interests encompass Mathematical General Relativity, Black Hole Physics, Gravitational Wave Theory, and Quantum Field Theory in Curved Spacetime. I am passionate about leveraging both analytical and computational techniques to unravel the complexities of our universe. I am very much passionate about the mathematical structure of General Relativity, and I aspire to contribute to the field through rigorous research and collaboration.

My Worldline : Experience

June 2025 - Present (Anticipated May 2026)

Master's Thesis: Asymptotic Symmetries, Conformal Structure of Spacetime and Gravitational Memory

AEI Potsdam & IUCAA Pune | Dr Sajad Aghapour & Prof. Emeritus Naresh Dadhich

Supervised by Dr. Sajad Aghapour (AEI, Potsdam) and Prof. Naresh Dadhich (IUCAA Pune, Until recent passing).

Investigating the Bondi-Metzner-Sachs (BMS) group of asymptotic symmetries in General Relativity and its physical manifestation: the Gravitational Memory Effect. Employing geometric algebra and symbolic computation (xAct) to analyze gravitational wave spacetimes and their asymptotic structure. We analyze the higher dimension cases in the a Conformal Gaussian Null coordinate and Bondi like coordinates. We developed an xAct based framework to compute and analyse higher dimensional spacetimes obeying Einstein's equatiions and it matches with that of the existing literature. Later in the thesis, we tried to understand these asymptotic symmetries and memory effect in framework of Covaraint Phase Space formalism. We would like to provide a covariant phase space description of charges associated and its relation to memory effect. Currently we are trying also trying to understand these in conjunction with the multipole moments of the source, and we focus on Kerr family of Black Holes as our test case.

Summer 2024 (3 Months)

DAAD WISE Research Fellow

Max Planck Institute for Gravitational Physics (AEI), Potsdam, Germany

Investigated gravitational analogues of optical effects, specifically the Gravitational Faraday and Spin Hall Effects, in the weak field regime, like that of a gravitational wave metric. I tried to calculate these for an interesting solution of Gravitational Bessel waves. This involved studying the propagation of polarized light rays in curved spacetime using the Mathisson-Papapetrou-Dixon equations and the geometric optics approximation in General Relativity. We developed a symbolic computation for the Fermi-Normal coordinate system using the xAct framework to facilitate these calculations. I further tried to explore the relation to topological phases in quantum mechanics, such as the Berry phase, in the context of curved spacetime and its still in my list of curious things, unchecked.

Fall 2025 - Present

Covariant Analysis of Gravitoelectromagnetism

IUCAA Pune | Prof. Emeritus Naresh Dadhich

Performed a covariant analytical study of gravitoelectromagnetism in perfect fluid spacetimes using the 1+3 (threading) decomposition relative to a timeline congruence. I developed a rigorous geometric understanding using tensorial methods and differential forms to analyze the decomposition of Weyl curvature into gravitoelectric and gravitomagnetic components.

The project investigated duality-like structures between these sectors, clarifying mathematical analogies to electromagnetic duality in curved spacetime. I further studied how the gravitoelectromagnetism system depends on fluid flow kinematics (expansion, shear, vorticity, acceleration) and examined the equation structure across cosmological and stationary spacetimes to identify conditions for simplifications or degeneracies.
